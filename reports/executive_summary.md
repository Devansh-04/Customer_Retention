# Executive Summary

The analysis shows that the D2C fashion brand has a meaningful customer base, but its revenue and retention behavior differ clearly across customer value segments.

The Power BI dashboard highlights that Premium Value and High Value customers contribute the strongest revenue. These segments also show stronger repeat purchase behavior, making them the most important groups for retention-focused strategy.

The Promo Dependency vs Retention analysis shows that not all valuable customers require the same level of discounts. Some high-value customers continue to show strong retention even with lower promotional reliance. These customers are suitable for gradual discount reduction. In contrast, low-value and promo-sensitive customers may still require controlled promotional offers to maintain volume.

The geographic analysis identifies regions where customers show strong average spend and lower promo dependency. These regions represent organic brand pull and should be prioritized for future acquisition campaigns.

The category funnel suggests that some product categories act as entry-point categories, while others are more strongly associated with repeat purchase behavior. The brand should use entry categories for acquisition and retention categories for loyalty-building campaigns.

The recommended strategy is not to eliminate discounts entirely, but to shift from broad promotions to segment-based promotional control. Premium and High Value customers should receive loyalty benefits, early access, and personalized offers instead of frequent direct discounts. Promo-dependent customers should receive conditional offers such as minimum-order discounts or category-specific promotions.

This approach allows the brand to reduce discount dependency, protect margins, and retain its most valuable customers without risking a sudden drop in sales volume.
