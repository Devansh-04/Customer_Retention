# Retention Playbook

## 1. Promotional Sunset Plan

### Target Segment
Premium Value and High Value customers with high previous purchases, high review ratings, and lower promo dependency.

### Reasoning
These customers already show strong repeat purchase behavior and contribute the highest revenue. Since their buying behavior is not fully dependent on discounts, they are suitable for gradual discount reduction.

### Action Plan
- Month 1: Reduce promo frequency by 25% for Premium Value customers.
- Month 2: Replace direct discounts with early access, free shipping, or loyalty benefits.
- Month 3: Extend the same strategy to High Value customers if retention does not drop significantly.

### Metrics to Track
- Retention Rate
- Average Spend
- Repeat Purchase Count
- Promo Dependency %
- Revenue per Customer

### Risk
Removing discounts too quickly may reduce purchase frequency or customer satisfaction. Therefore, the strategy should be tested gradually instead of applying it to all customers at once.

---

## 2. Segments That Should Continue Receiving Promotions

### Target Segment
Low Value customers and customers with high promo dependency but low previous purchases.

### Reasoning
These customers appear more price-sensitive and may not purchase without discounts. Removing discounts from this group may reduce short-term sales volume.

### Action Plan
- Continue promotional offers, but make them conditional.
- Use minimum order value discounts.
- Avoid blanket discounts.
- Give category-specific offers instead of site-wide offers.

### Risk
Continuing discounts may protect volume but can reduce margins and create long-term discount dependency.

---

## 3. Ideal Customer Profile

The ideal customer is a Premium or High Value customer with:
- High previous purchases
- High average spend
- Good review rating
- Low promo dependency
- Consistent purchase behavior across categories

### Business Interpretation
This customer type is valuable because they generate revenue without requiring heavy promotional spending. The brand should acquire more customers with similar behavior through targeted campaigns in high-spend, low-promo regions.

---

## 4. Final Recommendation

The brand should not stop promotions completely. Instead, it should move from broad discounting to segment-based promotional control.

Premium and High Value customers should receive fewer direct discounts and more loyalty-based benefits. Low Value and promo-dependent customers should receive controlled, conditional promotions. This protects margins while reducing the risk of losing sales volume.
